Our Vision
What we strive toward

To be a vibrant, holy, and welcoming Catholic community — rooted in the communion of the Most Holy Trinity, growing in authentic discipleship, and bearing courageous witness to Jesus Christ and His Church in Kuala Langat and beyond.

We aspire to be a parish where every baptised soul — from the oldest parishioner to the newest catechumen — finds a true spiritual home, sustained by the sacraments and enlivened by charity.

✝
Our Mission
How we live it out
Liturgy & Worship — To celebrate the Holy Sacrifice of the Mass and the sacraments with reverence, beauty, and full, conscious participation.
Community & Charity — To build a parish family rooted in solidarity, fraternal correction, and genuine care for one another in the spirit of the early Church.
Service to the Poor — To serve the poorest and most vulnerable in Kuala Langat, recognising Christ in the face of every person in need.
Evangelisation — To proclaim the Good News of salvation through Jesus Christ with courage, joy, and fidelity to the Magisterium.
Our Story

A Journey of Faith

From eleven Catholic families gathered by a river to a thriving parish of over 150 families — this is the story of God's providence at work.

The Church of Our Lady of Fatima — Our Humble Beginning

The Catholic community of Kuala Langat began with just 11 Catholic families. They gathered in a modest riverside bungalow known as The Church by the River — the Church of Our Lady of Fatima — which faithfully served the faithful for nearly four decades.

🏠
1975
📄
1976

Formal Canonical Registration

The parish was formally registered under the Archdiocese of Kuala Lumpur, establishing its canonical identity and placing it within the wider communion of the Church in Malaysia.

Decades of Sacrifice & Generosity

Through years of prayer, sacrifice, and the generous giving of the faithful, the parishioners raised an extraordinary RM 3 million — a testament to the power of a community united in charity and hope.

💜
1980s–2000s
⛪
2013

New Church Solemnly Opened — 24 November 2013

On the Solemnity of Christ the King, the new Church of Sts. Peter and Paul was opened. The Archdiocese bestowed its unique name because "both Peter and Paul are pillars of the Church" — making it the only church in Peninsular Malaysia bearing this joint apostolic name.

Holy Door of Mercy — Extraordinary Jubilee Year

During the Extraordinary Jubilee Year of Mercy (2016) proclaimed by Pope Francis, the Church of Sts. Peter and Paul was honoured as one of five designated Holy Door of Mercy churches in Peninsular Malaysia — a profound grace recognised by the Holy See.

Today

A Flourishing Catholic Community

Today, Sts. Peter & Paul Banting is home to over 150 families, with Holy Mass celebrated 5 times per week in English, Bahasa Malaysia, and Tamil. Under the pastoral care of Fr. Eugene Benedict, the parish continues to grow in holiness, service, and love.

Saint Peter

Feast Day: 29 June
Rock of the Church & First Pope

Born Simon Bar-Jonah, a fisherman from Galilee, he was chosen by Our Lord and renamed Cephas — Peter, meaning "rock." Christ declared: "Upon this rock I will build my Church, and the gates of Hades will not prevail against it" (Matthew 16:18), constituting Peter as the visible head of the Apostolic College.

The first Vicar of Christ, Peter received the keys of the Kingdom of Heaven. He was martyred for the Faith in Rome under Emperor Nero, crucified upside-down by his own request, deeming himself unworthy to die as his Lord and Saviour did.

Saint Paul

Feast Day: 29 June
Apostle to the Nations

Born Saul of Tarsus, a Pharisee zealous for the Law, he was dramatically converted through a personal encounter with the Risen Lord on the road to Damascus (Acts 9). Baptised by Ananias, he was set apart by the Holy Spirit for his extraordinary missionary vocation.

Paul undertook three great missionary journeys throughout the Mediterranean world, established numerous local churches, and authored fourteen epistles that form the doctrinal bedrock of Catholic theology. He was beheaded in Rome under Emperor Nero, giving the supreme witness for Christ and His Gospel.

The Church Building

A Sacred Space
for the Faithful

Opened on 24 November 2013, the Church of Sts. Peter and Paul is one of the most modern Catholic churches in Malaysia — a luminous, soaring structure built to serve the People of God in Kuala Langat for generations to come.

Entirely funded by the generosity and sacrifice of the parish community over decades, the building cost RM 3 million — a monument not of wealth, but of faith, perseverance, and love for God and His Church.

Rev. Fr. Eugene Benedict

Fr. Eugene Benedict serves as Parish Priest of the Church of Sts. Peter and Paul, Banting, incardinated in the Metropolitan Archdiocese of Kuala Lumpur. He shepherds a diverse, multilingual congregation with pastoral warmth, fidelity to the Sacred Tradition, and a profound love for the sacramental life of the Church.

"Dear brothers and sisters in Christ, welcome to our spiritual home here in Banting. Like our holy patrons Peter and Paul — the rock upon whom the Church is built, and the tireless herald of the Gospel to all nations — we too are called to be steadfast in faith and courageous in witness."

Under his pastoral leadership, the parish has grown in its sacramental life, active ministries, catechetical formation, and service to the wider community of Kuala Langat. His door is always open to the faithful he serves.

Our Diocese

Metropolitan Archdiocese
of Kuala Lumpur

The Church of Sts. Peter and Paul, Banting, is a parish of the Metropolitan Archdiocese of Kuala Lumpur — one of the most vibrant Catholic local Churches in Southeast Asia.

The Archdiocese was erected as the Diocese of Kuala Lumpur by Blessed Pope Pius XII on 25 February 1955, and elevated to the rank of a Metropolitan Archdiocese on 18 December 1972. Its territory encompasses Selangor, Negeri Sembilan, Pahang, Terengganu, and the Federal Territories of Kuala Lumpur and Putrajaya.

Our parish lives out the Archdiocesan vision of "A New Way of Being Church" — rooted in the Communion of the Trinity, in solidarity with all peoples, and witnessing Christ with joy.
